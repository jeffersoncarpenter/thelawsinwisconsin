About
Today we elect congressmen and women every few years. These people alone may change what laws are in place. Our representative democracy was designed long ago by wise people. Unfortunately, they did not have the Internet back then.
Like all politicians, we promise change. However, we do not promise to change this law or that law - we decidedly do not have stances on most political issues. Instead, we promise to change the way laws get made.
We will host an online database of the statutes currently in effect. If you are a registered voter, you will have the following abilities:
  • assign someone as your congressperson, giving them your vote
  • directly vote on bills
  • change any laws, and submit your bill for approval
As soon as any submitted change gets votes from 2/3 of the registered population, it is immediately signed into law. We will soon begin to build a rough draft of that website right here.
After election, we will initialize the website so that current members of congress carry the votes of their constituents. Then you can all log in and do whatever you want.
The website itself is open source at https://github.com/jeffersoncarpenter/thelawsinwisconsin
